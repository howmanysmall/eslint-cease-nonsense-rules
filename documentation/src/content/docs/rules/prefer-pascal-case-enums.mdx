Require PascalCase for enum names and enum members.

## Rule details :badge[Suggestion]{variant=note}

This rule reports any enum name or enum member whose text does not already match the plugin's PascalCase conversion.

It checks regular identifier members like `Red` and string-literal members like `'foo'`. String-literal members that
start with a digit are ignored.

<Aside type="tip" title="No auto-fix">
	The rule reports casing problems, but it does not rename your enums for you.
</Aside>

## Examples

<RuleExample type="fail" title="Incorrect">
		### Mixed casing

        	<Code
        		code={[
        			"enum colors {",
        			"\tRED,",
        			"\tblue,",
        			"}",
        			"",
        			"enum user_role {",
        			"\tadmin_user,",
        			"}",
        		].join("\n")}
        		lang="ts"
        	/>
    </RuleExample>

<RuleExample type="pass" title="Correct">
		### PascalCase enums

        	<Code
        		code={[
        			"enum Color {",
        			"\tRed,",
        			"\tBlue,",
        			"\tDarkBlue,",
        			"}",
        			"",
        			"enum UserRole {",
        			"\tAdminUser,",
        			"}",
        		].join("\n")}
        		lang="ts"
        	/>
    </RuleExample>

## Notes

Single-letter names like `X` or `A` are allowed. Acronym-style names such as `UDim2` and `CFrame` are also allowed when
they already match PascalCase.

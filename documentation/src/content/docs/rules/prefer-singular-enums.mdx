Reports TypeScript enum names that look plural.

## Rule details :badge[Suggestion]{variant=note}

Enums describe one selected member at a time, so this rule expects singular names.

It catches regular plurals like `Colors`, acronym plurals like `IDs`, and several irregular plurals like `People` and
`Children`. It also checks the last word in names such as `UserStatuses` and `USER_STATUSES`.

Some words that end in `s` are treated as singular and are allowed, including `Status`, `Alias`, `Analysis`, `Series`,
and `Species`.

<Aside type="tip" title="No fixer">
	The rule reports the enum declaration only. Rename the enum and its references with your editor or refactor tool.
</Aside>

## Examples

<RuleExample type="fail" title="Incorrect">
	<Code
		code={[
			"enum Colors {",
			"\tRed,",
			"\tBlue,",
			"}",
			"",
			"enum People {",
			"\tAdmin,",
			"\tUser,",
			"}",
			"",
			"enum USER_STATUSES {}",
		].join("\n")}
		lang="tsx"
	/>
</RuleExample>

<RuleExample type="pass" title="Correct">
	<Code
		code={[
			"enum Color {",
			"\tRed,",
			"\tBlue,",
			"}",
			"",
			"enum Person {",
			"\tAdmin,",
			"\tUser,",
			"}",
			"",
			"enum Status {}",
			"enum ID {}",
		].join("\n")}
		lang="tsx"
	/>
</RuleExample>
